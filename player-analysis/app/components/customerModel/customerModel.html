CustomerModel

Customer Model defines the multi-dimensional segmentation layers which comprises of Customer Lifecycle Stages:

  • Customer Longevity on the system
  • customer activity status
  • customer transactional behavior
  • customer affinities and preferences
  • customer quality and Customer value

Customer model approach provides 360-degree analysis of customer behavior and its impact on value creation. This helps in knowing why to talk,
when to talk, how to talk and what to talk to customers making whole database marketing a live and dynamic process.

Lifecycle Stages

Customer Life Cycle Segments shows the complete customer journey from Registration-to-Dormant how the value of customers differs at each level of their lifecycle stages. Customer life segments shows how a customer value peaks at active stage. This view provides a high level understanding of how your database distribution is and what are the future value you can expect from your customer database.
   

Segmentation Layers

FalconDive Proprietary segmentation layers provides a deep insight on activity levels of customers, on their Recency (when they made the last transaction), Latency (average number days between two successive transactions), frequency ( number of transactions in a period) and customer monetary value. Segmentation layer provides an analytical view on how customer migrates from one cluster to other through transition matrix created for customer transactional behavior tracking.

Customer Barcode Segments

 

Customer Barcode is a proprietary model which is a unique persona defined to each customer in the system.
Each customer will carry a Bar-code which contains a string of 18 attributes which contains:

  • Customer Demographics (Age Group, Gender, Market, Source of Traffic)
  • Customer First Touch (Channel, Deposit option, Platform, Store, Product, Sub product, Game Type, Game Variant)
  • Customer Info (Lifecycle Stages, Customer Age)
  • Customer Preferences (Preferred Channel, Platform, Product, Sub Product, Opportunity)
  • Customer transactional Behavior ( RFM, Customer Quality)

Value Segments

 

Customer Value segments are created to decide the size and level of investment which can be made for a customer to remain active.
Value segments drives the estimates of overall reinvestment rate for a customer. This also helps in defining the service levels required for customer satisfaction.
